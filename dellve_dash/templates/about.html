About Dellve

Dellve Deep is designed to quantify the performance of a GPU-enabled High Performance Computing server. The main tools that is packaged
with Dellve are benchmarks and stress tools that uses operations from Nvidiaâ€™s cuDNN library.

Training full DNNs for benchmarking is not feasible due to the wide range of DNN applications and architectures. Due to this, we target
low level DNN operations, and we leverage cuDNN due to its usage in popular frameworks such as Caffe, TensorFlow, and Torch.



Benchmarks vs Stress Tools

Benchmarks are meant to compare the performance of different GPUs, while stress tools are designed to determine the stability
of the GPU subsystem.

To achieve this, benchmarks have fixed problem sizes that keep the comparisons fair between benchmark runs across different GPUs.
On the other hand, stress tools have dynamic problem sizes generated to match the user-specified memory allocation. Once memory
is allocated, the stress tools will push the GPUs to 100% core utilization for a configurable amount of time.



CuDNN Operations

The current implementation of Dellve utilizes four fundamental operations for the purpose of running benchmarks and stress tools.
We also include both forward and backward propagations for each operation.

  • Activation
  • Convolution
  • Pooling
  • Softmax
